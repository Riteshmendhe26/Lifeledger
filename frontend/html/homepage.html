Why It Matters

Every 9 minutes, a new person is added to the national transplant waiting list.

17 individuals lose their lives each day due to the shortage of available organs.

One organ donor can save up to 8 lives and improve the lives of 75 others through eye and tissue donation.

FAQs

Anyone, regardless of age or medical history, can register as a potential donor. Your eligibility will be determined at the time of death based on medical criteria.

Organ matching is based on factors such as blood type, size, urgency, and location. Personal characteristics like gender, race, income, or social status are never considered.

Deceased donation occurs only after death is declared based on strict medical criteria. Registries are checked for prior consent, or consent is obtained from next of kin.

Transplant professionals follow national policies to determine organ suitability and recipient matching based on urgency and compatibility.
